Tenants Control Housing Now.

Right now, thousands of rent-stabilized apartments in NYC sit vacant. The reason? Landlords are leveraging units as ransom against local government to end rent stabilization in New York state. How is this possible?

CHIP, so-called "Community Housing Improvement Program," is an organization of 4,000 landlords who hoard over 400,000 rent-stabilized buildings in NYC. Rent stabilization keeps families in their homes, the bare minimum for a liveable city. Landlords' practice of warehousing, or holding vacant apartments, intentionally creates a crisis to squeeze the working class — already battling an ongoing pandemic, inflation, and corporate greed — for every penny while landlords sit on their assets.

So how do we fix this?
The fix must come from working class tenant power. The laws governing rent-stabilized apartments in New York City were written by the state government. In the three years since HSTPA has been passed, several of its protections have already been reversed; the Rent Guidlines Board has more than doubled the maximum percentage for rent increase on a renewed lease, and groups like CHIP are waging a fight in the Supreme Court to overturn further protections.

The cowards and cronies in Albany will only keep these protections in the face of massive, organized public resistance. Legal protections like those in the HSTPA do not sufficiently keep us safe. We must directly combat the control that landlords hold over our housing. Talk to your neighbors about forming a tenant association, look up your local tenant union or eviction defense group, and start dreaming of and working towards a world where not only CHIP, but all landlords, are obsolete. We are the only ones who can change the city's relationship to housing and bring about a world in which working class New Yorkers control our own housing and our own destiny.
